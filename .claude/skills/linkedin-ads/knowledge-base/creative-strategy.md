# Creative Strategy - Angle by Awareness Stage

Match the creative to where the buyer's head is. The same offer needs different creative at each stage.

## Problem-aware (TOF)
The buyer feels the pain but hasn't named a solution category.
- **How to approach:** hold up a mirror to the problem. Make them feel seen, don't sell.
- **Best angles:** trigger events, the main problem stated plainly, the goal/job-to-be-done, the daily frustration.
- **Formats:** thought-leader ads, single image with a sharp headline, short video.
- **CTA:** soft - read, watch, follow. Not "book a demo."

## Solution-aware (MOF)
They know solutions exist and are comparing approaches.
- **How to approach:** show the contrast between the old way and the new, and prove it.
- **Best angles:** use cases, before/after, comparisons, social proof, case studies.
- **Formats:** document ads, case-study single images, testimonial creative.
- **CTA:** medium intent - read the case study, see how it works.

## Product-aware (BOF)
They know you and are deciding.
- **How to approach:** reduce risk and make the next step obvious.
- **Best angles:** specific outcomes with numbers, testimonials, client logos, guarantees, direct offer.
- **Formats:** lead-form single images, direct-response creative.
- **CTA:** hard - book a demo, get the guide, start a trial.

## Angle categories to pull from
Trigger events, the core problem, goals/JTBD, pains with the solution, features, use cases, benefits, selfish desires, case studies, testimonials, ratings/awards, client logos. Map each to the stage where it fits and rotate so the audience doesn't fatigue on one angle.
