# Running LinkedIn Ads - The Essentials

A working foundation for B2B LinkedIn Ads. Enough to set up well and avoid the common ways money leaks.

## The full-funnel framework
Organize everything around three stages. Most accounts fail by running only the bottom and wondering why volume and cost are bad.

- **TOF (problem-aware)** - cold prospecting. Educational content, pain-point validation, thought-leadership. Goal: cheap reach and engagement with the right people.
- **MOF (solution-aware)** - retarget engaged users. Case studies, comparisons, demos. Goal: move interest toward intent.
- **BOF (product-aware)** - retarget high-intent. Direct CTA, social proof, lead forms. Goal: capture demand.

A rough starting split for a new account: lead with TOF + a BOF retargeting layer, add MOF as engagement builds. Retargeting is cheap and high-return; never skip it.

## Account structure
- **Campaign groups by stage and persona**, not one mixed bucket. Even with four campaigns, separate by intent so a strong retargeting campaign doesn't mask failing prospecting.
- **One promise per campaign.** Different audience or message = different campaign.
- **Naming convention** so reporting stays readable, e.g. `[Region] | [Persona] | [Stage]`.

## Targeting
- **Job function + seniority** scales better and costs less than long job-title lists, but needs ongoing negative filtering of irrelevant titles.
- **Company size and industry** to fit your ICP.
- **Turn Audience Expansion OFF. Turn the LinkedIn Audience Network OFF.** Both quietly spend on the wrong people. This is the single most common audit finding.
- Keep audiences in a sensible range - large enough to deliver, tight enough to stay relevant. Split large audiences into homogeneous segments rather than one broad blob.

## Bidding
- Start on automated/maximum-delivery bidding while you gather data, then move to manual CPC for control once you see how the audience responds.
- Watch cost per result, not cost per click. A cheap click that never converts is expensive.

## Creative
- The headline is the creative (see copywriting.md). One idea per ad.
- Rotate evenly at launch so each ad gets data, then let the best ones run.
- Diversify formats - different ad types compete in different auctions, which can lower cost.

## Measurement
- Track real pipeline, not just in-platform form fills. B2B deals close over weeks - reconcile against your CRM.
- Run a weekly demographic check: who actually saw and clicked the ads. Exclude the segments that don't match your ICP.

## Pre-launch checklist
- Conversion tracking (Insight Tag / conversions) installed and firing
- Audience built, Audience Expansion and Audience Network OFF
- Campaign group, naming, and budgets set
- At least 3-4 creatives per campaign, copy cleared the anti-slop audit
- UTMs on destination URLs
- Everything starts PAUSED; review, then enable
