# Ad Copywriting - Formulas, Voice of Customer, Process

## Rule #1: write from the customer's mouth, not the product's
The biggest mistake is using brand/marketing language instead of how the customer actually talks.
- Product voice: "Real-time cash visibility across all entities."
- Customer voice: "I used to spend 6-8 hours a week on this. Now it takes 15 minutes."

The customer voice wins every time because it mirrors what the buyer is already thinking. Gather it before writing a line.

## Voice of customer: where to get it
Ranked by quality:
1. G2 / Capterra reviews - real "before" and "after" in the user's words
2. Sales and discovery call notes - how they describe the problem before they know the solution
3. Testimonials and case studies - curated, but real language with numbers
4. Support tickets and onboarding feedback
5. Reddit / LinkedIn threads where the ICP discusses the problem
6. Competitor reviews - what people wish the competitor did better

Extract: pain phrases, outcome phrases, specific numbers, and emotional words.

## The headline is the creative
On the best B2B ads, the headline text is the main visual - bold sans-serif, one sentence, taking up most of the image, readable at thumbnail size on a solid background. It delivers the message even when nobody reads the body. Bright backgrounds pop against LinkedIn's white feed. Put one idea on the image, a small logo, optionally one accent color. No stock photos, no feature lists.

## Six headline formulas

1. **Feeling** - "[Desired state], not [current pain]." → "Insights, not anxiety." Best at TOF / problem-aware.
2. **Conversation** - a short scene or dialogue the ICP has lived. → "Board: 'What's our cash position?' / You: 'Give me a day.'" Best at TOF; great for normalized problems.
3. **Contrast** - "No more [pain]. Just [outcome]." or "[old] → [new]." → "No more bank portals. Just one dashboard." Best at MOF / solution-aware.
4. **Shame** - frame the status quo as outdated, lightly. → "It's 2026. Why is your forecast still in a spreadsheet?" Best at MOF.
5. **Stat interrupt** - one dramatic, specific number as the visual. → "6-8 hrs → 15 min." Any stage. Numbers must be real, not round-and-made-up.
6. **Pain + outcome** - "[Pain verb] the [old thing], [outcome verb] [result]." → "Ditch the spreadsheets, scale faster." Best at MOF-BOF.

## The process
1. **Gather VOC** - at least five real customer quotes before writing.
2. **One idea per ad.** If you're saying two things, make two ads.
3. **Pick the formula** that fits the idea and the funnel stage.
4. **Write the image text first** - the one sentence that must stop the scroll on its own.
5. **Write the body copy** - expand with context and proof, end on a clear next step.
6. **Write the headline field** - support the image, don't repeat it (social proof or an outcome stat).
7. **Audit** against [anti-ai-slop.md](anti-ai-slop.md) before it ships.

## Common mistakes
- Brand language instead of customer language.
- Feature headlines instead of pain/outcome headlines.
- Too many ideas on one ad.
- Generic pain questions ("Struggling with X?") instead of a specific lived moment.
- Vague outcomes ("make smarter decisions") instead of a specific result.
