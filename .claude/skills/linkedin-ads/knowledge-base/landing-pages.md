# Landing Page Strategy for LinkedIn Ads

Landing pages are the other half of ad performance. A great ad sending traffic to a bad page wastes budget. Each funnel stage and awareness level needs a different landing page approach.

## Landing Pages by Awareness Stage

### Problem-Aware (TOF traffic)

These visitors are early - they're exploring a pain, not shopping for a product.

**Page direction:**
- Problem validation and user empathy - show you understand their world
- Outcome-focused testimonials or stories (not product features)
- Introductory brand messaging - build familiarity, not hard sell
- Before/After transformation visuals - "this is your life now vs what it could be"

**Where to send them:** Homepage or content hub. NOT a demo page.

### Solution-Aware (MOF traffic)

These visitors know what they need. They're comparing options.

**Page direction:**
- Value proposition clarity: strong headline + benefit + visual
- ROI snapshots or short case studies
- Key differentiators vs competitors (comparison tables work well here)
- Easy ways to get started: "Try free," "Book demo," "See it live"
- Optional: feature checklist, integration matrix

**Where to send them:** Solution pages, comparison pages, feature-specific landing pages.

### Product-Aware (BOF traffic)

These visitors are close to deciding. They need reassurance and a push.

**Page direction:**
- Specific benefits for their persona/industry
- Strong social proof: testimonials, results, recognizable client logos
- Product visuals or interactive tours
- Time-based incentive or limited offers
- Final reassurance: support quality, security certifications, outcome guarantees

**Where to send them:** Dedicated demo page or free trial page. Low friction.

## Landing Page Best Practices

**Structural rules:**
- CTA on the landing page must match the CTA on the ad
- Landing page must be relevant to the ad copy and creative
- Each campaign type should ideally have its own landing page
- At minimum, have 2 pages per campaign: one for prospecting, one for retargeting

**Competitor-focused pages:**
- Build comparison landing pages for Solution-Aware audiences
- Include comparison tables, feature checklists
- Add customer testimonials specifically from switchers - "Why [Company] moved from [Competitor] to us"
- Embed customer video reviews if available

**Retargeting-specific pages:**
- Display USPs and social proof prominently
- Show customer logos and specific results
- Make the CTA frictionless - pre-filled forms, short Lead Gen Forms
- Personalize by industry or use case if possible

## Lead Gen Form Optimization

LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms bypass the landing page entirely. When to use them:

**Best for:**
- BOF campaigns targeting warm audiences
- When the main friction is "leaving LinkedIn" (users stay on-platform)
- When your landing page conversion rate is below 5%

**Form design rules:**
- Keep to 3-5 fields maximum
- Use LinkedIn auto-fill fields (name, email, job title, company) - reduces friction
- Custom questions should be minimal - one qualifying question at most
- Thank-you message should set expectations: "We'll reach out within 24 hours"
- Document ads + Lead Gen Forms for demo page visitors can yield 2.5x higher demo-to-close rate

**When NOT to use Lead Gen Forms:**
- When lead quality is the problem (forms can generate low-intent leads)
- When you need deeper qualification (use website forms with more fields instead)
- When the landing page experience IS the selling point (demos, free tools, interactive content)
