# ABM Campaign Strategy for LinkedIn Ads

How to run Account-Based Marketing campaigns on LinkedIn effectively. Based on $25M+ in LinkedIn ad spend data.

## Core Principle

ABM campaigns are not just about targeting your ideal accounts - they're about reaching them. A perfectly targeted list that only reaches 15% of its companies is wasting 85% of its potential.

## ABM Campaign Types

| Type | Accounts | Targeting Method | Best For |
|------|----------|-----------------|----------|
| 1:1 | Single company | Add company name directly to targeting | Whale accounts, strategic deals |
| 1:few | Up to 10-20 companies | Company list upload | Named account programs |
| 1:many | Multiple companies (industry, segment) | Company list or native targeting | Vertical campaigns, segment-level ABM |

**1:1 campaigns:** Simply add the company name to your targeting. The simplest approach but only works for large enough companies with enough LinkedIn members.

**1:few and 1:many campaigns:** Use list-based targeting for better optimization and performance tracking. You can technically add companies one by one, but lists unlock better analytics.

## Lists: Company vs Contact

### Company Lists (Recommended)

Company lists are generally preferred for ABM campaigns for three reasons:

1. **Easier to obtain** - Contact lists require accurate business emails that match LinkedIn profiles
2. **Better matching** - LinkedIn matches company names more reliably than individual email addresses to profiles
3. **Less maintenance** - People constantly leave and join companies; company lists stay relevant longer

### Contact Lists

Contact lists can still be useful when you have a clear identification of the best people to reach at target companies. They allow more precise targeting of specific roles within accounts.

**When to use contact lists:**
- You have verified, high-quality business email lists
- You need to reach very specific individuals (e.g., named decision-makers)
- Your ABM motion requires person-level targeting

**Caveat:** Contact lists are harder to obtain for prospecting efforts and require frequent updates as people change jobs.

## List Sizes and Formats

| Parameter | Minimum | Recommended | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------------|-------|
| List rows (minimum for LinkedIn audience) | 300 | 1,000+ | LinkedIn requires 300-member minimum to launch |
| Company names for company lists | 300 rows | 1,000+ | LinkedIn suggests 1K names; 300 can work with good match rate |
| Emails for contact lists | 300 rows | 10,000+ | LinkedIn suggests 10K emails for successful matching |
| Minimum audience size to launch campaign | 300 members | 1,000+ | Below 300 = LinkedIn won't let you launch |

**Key detail:** LinkedIn doesn't require 1K unique company names. You can include duplicate company names with other info to fill 1K rows. The system deduplicates on match.

**Cold campaign minimum:** 15,000 audience members for cold ABM campaigns to give the algorithm enough room to optimize.

## Using Company Lists for Performance Analysis

One of the biggest advantages of company lists is the per-company performance reporting.

**How to access:**
1. Go to Audiences in Campaign Manager
2. Find your company list
3. Click on it to see the engagement report
4. Sort by impressions, clicks, or engagement

**What this tells you:**
- Which companies are receiving impressions (and which aren't)
- Engagement levels by company name
- Where to focus optimization efforts

**Optimization action:** If certain companies aren't receiving impressions, segment them into a separate campaign. This prevents LinkedIn from over-serving ads to the easiest-to-reach companies while ignoring others.

## ABM List Performance: Native vs List-Based Targeting

Real performance data comparison:

| Metric | Native Targeting | List-Level Targeting |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| CPM | Baseline | ~50% cheaper |
| Decision-maker interaction | Lower | Higher - more decision-makers engaged |
| Individual contributor interaction | Higher | Lower |

**Why list-based is cheaper:** ABM campaigns using company lists expose your ICP to your product at a lower distribution cost. The list provides LinkedIn with a clearer signal for ad delivery.

**Why decision-maker engagement is higher with lists:** The list-based targeting is more precise, reaching the right people within target companies rather than casting a wide net.

## Challenges of Large Lists

Large lists with a mix of company types and sizes cause problems:

**LinkedIn prioritizes ad spend on enterprises in mixed lists.** If you upload 3K companies of varying sizes, your budget might only reach 10% of them. One account reached only 15% of their account list after adding a large, unsegmented list.

**The fix:** Segment large lists into multiple smaller, more homogeneous lists:

| Segment | List | Campaign |
|---------|------|----------|
| Enterprise (500+ employees) | List A | Campaign A - higher budget per account |
| Mid-Market (200-500) | List B | Campaign B - moderate budget per account |
| SMB (0-200) | List C | Campaign C - efficient budget per account |

Or segment by:
- Industry vertical
- Geographic region
- Deal stage / pipeline priority
- Revenue tier

## ABM Campaign Structure

### For 1:1 Accounts (Whale Deals)

- Create dedicated campaigns per account (or per small cluster of 2-3 similar accounts)
- Use company name targeting directly
- Pair with contact list if you have verified emails for key stakeholders
- Run both decision-maker and influencer messaging
- Coordinate with sales team for timing and messaging

### For 1:Few Programs (Named Account Lists)

- Upload company list
- Segment into homogeneous sub-lists if mix of company sizes
- Create awareness + retargeting campaign pair per list segment
- Monitor per-company engagement report weekly
- Separate underserved companies into dedicated campaigns

### For 1:Many / Vertical ABM

- Use broader company lists (industry-based)
- Layer with job function + seniority targeting for precision
- Monitor demographics report for irrelevant titles - add negative filters
- Scale by splitting into region-specific or size-specific sub-campaigns

## ABM Budget Considerations

- 1:1 accounts: Higher cost per account is acceptable - the deal value justifies it
- 1:few: Budget should ensure each account gets meaningful impression share
- 1:many: Treat more like scaled prospecting with list-based precision
- Always monitor per-company reach - if budget is spread too thin, accounts won't get enough impressions to matter

## Account List Building Best Practices

Building the right account list is the foundation of ABM. Don't just pull a random list - build it strategically.

**Sources for building ABM lists:**
- Work with sales team to identify highest-value target accounts
- Analyze closed-won deals - what do your best customers have in common?
- Check late-stage pipeline - which account types consistently progress?
- Use intent data platforms to identify accounts actively researching your category
- Review competitor customer lists for poachable accounts

**Validation:** Cross-reference your list with LinkedIn to ensure the companies have enough employees on the platform to generate meaningful reach. Small companies (<50 employees) may not have enough LinkedIn members to serve ads to.

## ABM Frequency Management

**Target frequency:** ~3 impressions per week per person within target accounts. This keeps your brand visible without creating ad fatigue.

**When frequency gets too high:**
- Rotate creatives to prevent fatigue
- Expand the audience within the account (add more job functions or seniority levels)
- Pause and restart campaigns to reset delivery

**Impression capping via Company Engagement Feature:** LinkedIn's Company Engagement Feature allows you to create dynamic exclusion lists based on engagement thresholds. Use this to prevent over-serving specific accounts:

1. Set up Company Engagement tracking in Campaign Manager
2. Create a dynamic audience of companies that have hit 500+ impressions in the last 7 days
3. Add this audience as an exclusion to your ABM campaigns
4. Companies automatically rotate in and out of the exclusion as their impression count resets

**Adjusting the threshold:** Start at 500 impressions/7 days. If accounts are still seeing ad fatigue, lower to 300. If you need more reach, raise to 750. The right threshold depends on your account size and campaign aggressiveness.

## One-to-One Personalized ABM Ads

For whale accounts, create ads that include the target company's name and/or logo directly in the ad creative. This hyper-personalization dramatically increases engagement.

**Performance:** Personalized 1:1 ABM ads see 5-10% CTR vs 0.5-1% standard CTR - a 5-10x improvement in click-through rate.

**Best practices:**
- Include the company name in the ad headline or creative image
- Reference the company's specific industry or known challenges
- Use dynamic creative tools to scale personalization across multiple accounts
- Works best for MarTech, SalesTech, and Customer Success tools where the value prop is clear per account

**Legal restriction:** Do not use personalized company name/logo ads in Germany - German privacy regulations restrict this type of targeting. This is a legal issue, not a LinkedIn policy issue.

## Ad Engagement as Sales Trigger

ABM ad engagement data is a valuable signal for the sales team. Use it to prioritize outreach.

**How to operationalize:**
- Track which companies and individuals are engaging with your ads
- Use tools like Fibbler to connect LinkedIn ad engagement data to your CRM
- Set up automated alerts for sales when target account engagement spikes
- Engagement signals include: ad clicks, video views (50%+), Lead Gen Form opens, multiple impressions in a short window

**Sales coordination:**
- Share weekly engagement reports with the sales team
- When a target account shows high engagement, sales should initiate outreach within 48 hours
- The sales message should reference the problem/solution angle from the ad campaign (without mentioning they "saw the ad")
- Track whether sales-engaged accounts that also saw ads convert at higher rates than cold outreach alone

## ABM-Specific Remarketing Lists

Create separate remarketing audiences specifically for ABM accounts. Don't mix ABM remarketing with general remarketing.

**ABM remarketing audiences to build:**
- Image ad engagers from ABM campaigns (separate from general ad engagers)
- Video viewers (50%+) from ABM campaigns
- Website visitors from ABM company list (overlay company list with website visitor audience)

**Why separate:** ABM accounts warrant different messaging and higher CPAs than general remarketing. Mixing them dilutes your ability to customize messaging and measure ABM-specific performance.

## ABM Exclusion Best Practices

**Weekly ABM hygiene:**
- Check demographics report for each ABM campaign
- Exclude irrelevant job functions that are consuming budget (e.g., HR, IT support at target companies)
- Exclude competitors that may have matched your company list
- Exclude companies that have already become customers (move to customer expansion campaigns)

**Common exclusions for ABM:**
- Unpaid/intern seniority levels
- Job functions unrelated to your buyer persona
- Companies below your minimum deal size threshold
- Agencies and consulting firms (unless they're targets)

## ABM as Multi-Channel Orchestration

LinkedIn Ads are one part of a comprehensive ABM strategy - not the whole thing. The most effective ABM programs orchestrate multiple touchpoints.

**Channels to coordinate with LinkedIn ABM:**
- Sales outreach (email, phone, LinkedIn InMail) timed with ad campaigns
- Email marketing sequences targeting the same account list
- Events and webinars with personalized invitations to target accounts
- Direct mail for high-value 1:1 accounts
- Content syndication targeting the same companies

**Orchestration principle:** The prospect should encounter your brand 7-10 times across channels before the sales conversation. LinkedIn ads handle the awareness and middle-funnel touchpoints, while sales handles the direct engagement.

## Multi-Account ABM with Business Manager

When running ABM across multiple LinkedIn ad accounts (e.g., by region), use Business Manager to share audiences:
- Upload company list to one account, then share it across other accounts via Business Manager's "Share Audiences" feature
- If using HubSpot, send audience to one account, then distribute via Business Manager
- Match Audiences page in Business Manager shows all audiences, last modified date, and how many accounts are using each
- This eliminates the need to upload and maintain the same list in multiple accounts separately
